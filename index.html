About this database

This is a searchable, mobile- and desktop-friendly atlas of the 239 RNA virus species known to infect humans. RNA viruses — including influenza, measles, HIV, SARS-CoV-2, Ebola and dengue — cause a large share of the world's infectious-disease burden and dominate the list of emerging pandemic threats.

Each record draws on the peer-reviewed catalogue by Zhang et al. and includes ICTV taxonomy, genome type, envelope status, likely transmission routes, observed host range, and the date and place of the first reported human infection. Where a match exists, we also surface a spillover risk score — an estimate of a virus's potential to jump from animals to people.

Learning more. To understand why these viruses matter, pair this database with the WHO and CEPI reports in Sources. The WHO framework explains how the world prioritises pathogen families for research; CEPI's strategy lays out the plan to develop vaccines against them within 100 days of an outbreak.

For research and educational use. Data is reproduced "as is" from the sources below and may contain errors or omissions — always confirm against primary literature before relying on it. This site is not medical advice.
